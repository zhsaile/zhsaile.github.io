Test Prep

Language of the Test

infer :heavy_check_mark: To infer is to make a well informed guess. If you see Mr Y's car outside of the office, you might infer that he's in work.

perspective :heavy_check_mark: A way of regarding situations or topics. If you think that toys corrupt children's minds, then from your perspective a toy shop is an evil place.

rhetorical 修辞的 Rhetoric is the art of written or spoken communication. A hundred years ago, the English class was called Rhetoric. But nowadays if we say something is rhetorical, we usually mean that it's only good for talking. A rhetorical question is a question (such as "How could I be so stupid?") that's asked merely for effect with no answer expected.

foreshadow :heavy_check_mark: forecast, to foreshadow is to predict something or to give a hint of what is to come. Dark gray clouds foreshadow a thunderstorm.

abhor  :speaker: :heavy_check_mark: If you abhor something, it gives you a feeling of complete hatred. Chances are you abhor that kid who used to torture the frogs in biology class.

pledge :heavy_check_mark: promise, guarantee
